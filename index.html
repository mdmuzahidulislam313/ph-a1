Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh is pushing boundaries by backing new ideas and technologies. From startups to research hubs, it's all about turning fresh ideas into real solutions. Shark Tank Bangladesh shows how local talent gets recognised and supported.

Sustainability

Efforts in renewable energy, afforestation, and sustainable farming are shaping a greener tomorrow. Bangladesh is taking practical steps to balance development with environmental care.

Infrastructure

Modern roads, bridges, and urban projects are being developed to ease movement and connect communities, helping both people and businesses thrive in a fast-paced world.

Empowerment

By focusing on education, job training, and financial aid, Bangladesh is enabling people, especially women and underprivileged groups, to take charge of their future and actively contribute to the nation's progress.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Bangladesh is working to build a connected digital network, making online services and digital tools available for everyone, no matter where they live.

Green Energy

Solar and wind power projects are growing, bringing clean energy to homes and businesses, ensuring a healthier and more sustainable way forward.

Modern Infrastructure

Bangladesh is creating advanced facilities and infrastructure to support rapid economic growth and make the country globally competitive.

"I strongly believe that we can create a poverty-free world, if we want to.... In that kind of world, [the] only place you can see poverty is in the museum. When school children will be on a tour of the poverty museum, they will be horrified to see the misery and indignity of human beings. They will blame their forefathers for tolerating this inhuman condition to continue in a massive way."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
